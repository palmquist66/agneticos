# VetForge AI — Positioning

## Primary Angle

**Name:** The Insider Partner + Not Software, Service
**Statement:** VetForge AI builds AI-powered business services for veterinary practices — from inside one. Not another software subscription. A hands-on partner who delivers results and trains your team.
**Psychology:** Vets are skeptical of tech companies who don't understand their world. "Built inside a real hospital" is instant credibility — especially through buyer group referrals where peer trust drives purchasing. Positioning as a person who does the work (vs another SaaS login) addresses real subscription fatigue in this market. The name "VetForge" reinforces this — forging tools specifically for vets, not repurposing generic tech.
**Headline direction:** "We didn't study vet practices from the outside. We built this inside one."
**Best for:** Veterinary practices accessible through buyer group networks, where peer recommendation is the primary purchasing driver.

## Market Assessment

**Sophistication:** Stage 2-3 — Vet practices know AI exists and have heard the hype, but most haven't adopted it for business operations. Clinical AI (SOAP notes, diagnostics) is growing fast, but business-side AI automation is largely untouched. They've tried marketing agencies with mixed results.
**Transformation:** Practice goes from not doing SEO, posting social media once a month, and having no AI strategy → to having their website actively maintained and ranking in both Google and AI search, consistent on-brand social content, and a team that knows how to use AI day-to-day. They get the results of work they could never afford to hire for — without adding headcount or having to touch their website.
**Mechanism:** JP uses AI (Claude) as a force multiplier on the backend to deliver professional services that would normally require a full marketing team — and now *operates* the practice's website directly rather than handing over reports for someone else to implement. The client sees results, a monthly report, and full control (approval before changes go live + self-serve publishing) — never the AI tooling. On the training side, JP teaches the practice team to use AI for their own daily tasks.
**Primary alternative:** Hiring a vet marketing agency ($1,500-5,000/month, traditional approach, no AI automation), buying more software subscriptions (Shepherd, Digitail — clinical-focused, not business operations), or trying to figure out AI themselves (no time, steep learning curve).

## Competitive Landscape

**Competitors analyzed:**
- **Vet SaaS products** (Shepherd, Digitail, CoVet, HappyDoc) — Clinical AI tools (SOAP notes, transcription, diagnostics). $99-299/month subscriptions. They automate the clinical side, not the business side.
- **Vet marketing agencies** (GrowDVM, VeterinaryMarketing.ai, Thrive) — Traditional digital marketing (SEO, Google Ads, social media management) for vet practices. NOT doing AI automation of business operations. $1,500-5,000/month.
- **Generic AI automation agencies** (Lifebots.Co, etc.) — Serve all small businesses. $2,500-15,000 setup + $2,000-8,000/month retainers. Don't know anything about vet practices.
- **DIY AI tools** — Practice owner tries ChatGPT/Claude themselves. Too busy, inconsistent, no strategy behind it.

**Saturated claims (avoid):**
- "We grow your practice" — every vet marketing agency says this
- "AI-powered practice management" — the SaaS companies own this space
- "Full-service digital marketing" — generic, undifferentiated
- "Save time and money" — too vague without specifics

**White space identified:**
- Hands-on AI business automation consulting specifically for veterinary practices (nobody does this)
- Service model vs SaaS model — practices are drowning in subscriptions, they want a person
- Insider credibility — built inside a real hospital, not a tech lab
- Buyer group distribution — built-in referral channel that no competitor is using
- Business-side automation (SEO, content, admin) vs clinical AI (SOAP notes, diagnostics) — different problem, different buyer
- Brand name itself signals the niche — "VetForge" tells you who it's for before you read a word of copy

## Selection Rationale

"Not Software, Service" was merged in because the vet market is experiencing subscription fatigue from the flood of SaaS tools (Shepherd, Digitail, CoVet, ezyVet, etc.) — positioning as a person who does the work, not another login, directly addresses this pain. The Bandwidth Creator (Angle 2) is the strongest supporting message for content and sales conversations. ## Service Offerings

VetForge operates as a hands-on partner, not a report vendor — we do the work *and maintain it*, while the practice stays in control. Full tiers, pricing, and onboarding live in `projects/briefs/vetforge-ai-launch/2026-05-23_service-packaging.md`.

**The recurring spine — keeping the practice found.** We maintain the practice's website and keep it ranking in both legacy Google SEO and the AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews). AI-search rules shift constantly, so "we keep up so you don't have to, and keep you in the answer when a pet owner asks ChatGPT for a vet" is an inherently ongoing service — the core recurring justification.

**Two engagement models:**
1. **Operator** (Forge $1,200/mo · Foundry $2,200/mo) — we build, host, and maintain the site directly. GEO/SEO + AI-search optimization implemented for them, social content in brand voice, GBP, review responses, AI team training. Foundry adds done-with-you automation and strategy.
2. **Handoff** (Spark $600/mo) — for practices locked into a current web-dev contract. We provide strategy, brand-voice content, and developer-ready implementation specs; their developer ships it. Upgrades to Operator when the contract ends.

**On-ramp:** one-time **Website Rebuild** (from $2,500) onto a stack we control, AI-search-ready from day one. HPAH is the proof case. Flows straight into Operator maintenance.

**The client stays in control (all tiers):**
- We report on everything monthly — what changed, where they rank in Google + AI search, what moved
- Client picks approval mode — Review-first (preview + approve before anything goes live) or Auto-pilot
- Post-it-yourself publishing — client drops a blog or update in a shared folder; we optimize it for brand voice + AI search and publish it. No login, no CMS — reinforces "service, not software."

**AI enablement (differentiator + leverage):** beyond marketing, VetForge keeps the practice current on AI itself — short how-to videos and guides on using AI tools for everyday practice tasks, kept up to date as AI changes. Built once and shared across every client (one-to-many leverage), it also doubles as buyer-group authority content. Reinforces the "insider who keeps you ahead" positioning.

**Setup:** $500 one-time (brand voice profile build + baseline audit), waived if a Website Rebuild is part of the engagement. Month-to-month default; annual = 2 months free.
