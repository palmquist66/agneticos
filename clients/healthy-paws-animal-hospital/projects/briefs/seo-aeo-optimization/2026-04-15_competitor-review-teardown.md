## Recurring Themes Across All Competitors

### What Customers Praise (universal)
- Named veterinarian relationships (every competitor has a "star" vet)
- Staff warmth and patience with anxious pets
- Compassionate end-of-life care
- Long-term client loyalty (multiple reviewers mention years of service)

### Recurring Complaints
- **VCA:** Upselling, CareClub subscription complaints, pricing 8% above average
- **Dundee:** Front desk rudeness, high fees ($150/exam)
- **Pet Vet:** $150 same-day appointment fee, front desk arguing about services
- **Cary Grove:** Cost perceived as too high for some visits
- **HPAH:** Large/anxious dog handling (minor — only 2 reviews)

**Opportunity:** HPAH's pricing is perceived as reasonable while every competitor has pricing complaints. This is a genuine differentiator to highlight in review response templates and GBP posts.

## Review Generation Strategy

Based on the 8-10/month target:

1. **Ask trigger:** After every positive visit — especially dental cleanings, wellness exams, and puppy/kitten visits (high satisfaction moments)
2. **What to ask customers to mention:**
   - The specific service received (e.g., "dental cleaning", "wellness exam")
   - "Lake in the Hills" or their city name
   - Dr. Burgess by name (reinforces the personal brand signal)
   - The low-stress experience (direct-to-room, treats during procedures)
3. **Example ask script:** "If you have a moment, a Google review mentioning your experience with Dr. Burgess would really help other Lake in the Hills pet owners find us. Here's the link."
4. **Monthly tracking:** Check review count on the 1st of each month against the 8-10/month target
5. **Quick wins:** Follow up with clients who mention loving the visit — text/email the Google review link within 24 hours while the experience is fresh
