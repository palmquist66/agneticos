POST 19: Service Spotlight, Surgery Beyond Spay and Neuter
(Publish Monday or Tuesday)

Most owners think of surgery as something that happens at a referral hospital somewhere else. At Healthy Paws, we handle most surgical procedures right here in Lake in the Hills.

Mass removals, soft tissue surgery, dental extractions, foreign body removal, biopsies. These are routine for our team and our surgical suite is equipped to handle them. Your pet stays in our care from check-in through recovery, and you talk to the same vet who did the surgery when it is time to discuss what we found.

We run pre-surgical bloodwork in-house so results come back the same day, not three days later. We discuss costs and options before the procedure, not after. And we follow up personally to check on recovery, not with a system-generated email.

If your dog or cat needs surgery and you are trying to figure out where to go, give us a call. We will walk you through what we can handle, what would be better at a specialist, and what your options look like.

POST 20: Pet Care Tip, Fourth of July Prep
(Publish Wednesday or Thursday)

Fireworks season is coming. If your dog hides under the bed, shakes uncontrollably, or tries to bolt every July 4th, you have about three weeks to get ahead of it.

Anxiety meds work best when they are started before your dog is already terrified. Same for calming supplements and pheromone diffusers. If you have been thinking about trying something this year, the time to call us is now, not the morning of the fourth.

Some practical things you can do at home: walk your dog earlier in the day so they are tired and well-exercised before fireworks start. Close blinds and play soft music or TV to muffle the sound. Make sure your pet is wearing tags and their microchip information is current. More pets go missing on July 4th than any other day of the year.

If your dog has had a hard time in past years, let us help you build a plan. There is a lot we can do.

POST 22: Service Spotlight, In-House Laboratory and Diagnostics
(Publish Monday or Tuesday)

When your pet is sick, the last thing you want to hear is "we will get the results back in a few days."

At Healthy Paws, we run most diagnostic tests right here. Bloodwork, urinalysis, fecal exams, cytology. Results in minutes or hours, not days. That means we can start treatment the same day in most cases, instead of sending you home to wait while your pet stays uncomfortable.

In-house lab matters most when something is wrong. A dog who stops eating, a cat losing weight, a pet that is just not themselves. We can usually figure out what is going on during the visit instead of asking you to come back next week.

We also use digital X-ray, digital dental radiography, and ultrasound when we need to look deeper. Everything happens under one roof, with us.

If your pet has been feeling off and you are tired of waiting for answers, call us.

POST 23: Pet Care Tip, Tick Checks After Outdoor Adventures
(Publish Wednesday or Thursday)

McHenry County trails are gorgeous in June. They are also genuine tick territory. Veterans Acres, Three Oaks, Bull Valley, the Fox River Trail. All beautiful, all full of ticks once the weather warms up.

After every hike or long walk, do a tick check. Run your hands over your dog's body and pay extra attention to the ears, around the collar, between the toes, and the belly. Ticks are easy to miss in dense fur, especially the early-stage ones that are no bigger than a poppy seed.

If you find one, do not panic. Remove it with a tick removal tool or fine tweezers, pulling straight up close to the skin. Save the tick in a sealed bag in case we need to identify it later.

Year-round parasite prevention does most of the work, but no prevention is 100%. The check is the backup.

If you are not sure your pet is on the right prevention, or you find a tick and are not sure what to do, call us.

POST 24: The Single Exam Room Experience
(Publish Friday or Saturday)

One of the first things new clients notice at Healthy Paws is that we do not really have a waiting room.

When you arrive, you and your pet go straight to a private exam room. No crowded lobby. No anxious dog across from you barking at your cat. No sitting in the open while your pet pulls at the leash trying to leave. Just a calm room with floor-to-ceiling windows, the team coming to you, and a pace that fits your pet, not a corporate schedule.

Checkout happens in the same room. You are not standing at a front desk discussing your pet's health and your bill in front of strangers. Everything that needs to be private stays private.

For most of our clients, this is the first time a vet visit has felt manageable. For some, it is the first time their pet has actually seemed relaxed.

It is a small operational choice that changes how the whole visit feels. Come see for yourself. We welcome tours anytime.
