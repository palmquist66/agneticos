## What You're Doing Now: Reviews

Great that you're already working through these. Two things to keep in mind as you go:

### 1. Respond Faster Going Forward

Your current average response time is 44.6 days. Dundee responds in 2.3 days. The first 24-48 hours after someone leaves a review is when Google weighs the response most heavily for rankings. Once you're caught up on the backlog, set a daily 5-minute block — open GBP, respond to anything new from the last 24 hours.

### 2. Add Keywords to Every Response

Your responses are already the best in the market for warmth and personalization. The one thing missing: you mention **zero services and zero locations** in your responses. Every response is a chance to get "Lake in the Hills," "dental cleaning," "wellness exam," etc. indexed by Google.

**We put together 12 response templates as a starting point — use them as-is, or just borrow ideas and make them your own.** They're meant to be a guide, not a script. The main thing is weaving in a service mention and "Lake in the Hills" naturally, which your current responses don't do yet.

Here are a few examples to show what we mean:

**5-Star (example):**
> Thank you so much, [NAME]! We loved seeing [PET NAME] for their [SERVICE] at our Lake in the Hills clinic. Pet parents like you are why we do what we do. — Dr. Burgess

**4-Star (example):**
> Thanks for the thoughtful review, [NAME]. We're glad [PET NAME]'s [SERVICE] visit went well overall. If there's anything we could do better, don't hesitate to let us know. — Dr. Burgess, Healthy Paws Animal Hospital, Lake in the Hills

**Negative review (example):**
> [NAME], I'm sorry to hear this. I take it seriously and I'd like to understand what happened. Please reach out directly at (815) 322-5400 or service@hpah.org — I'll call you back the same day. — Dr. Burgess

The full set of 12 templates (3 per star rating) is in the Review Response Strategy doc we sent over. Feel free to tweak the wording to match how you'd naturally say things — the important part is getting a service keyword and "Lake in the Hills" into each response.

**Key rules for every response:**
- Use the reviewer's first name
- Use the pet's name (if they mentioned it)
- Mention the service they came in for (dental, wellness exam, etc.)
- Mention "Lake in the Hills" or their city naturally
- Keep it 2-3 sentences for positive reviews, 3-5 for negative
- Never argue with a negative review — offer to resolve offline with your phone number: (815) 322-5400

## What's Next: Getting More Reviews

Here's the honest math. You have 160 reviews. Dundee has 476 and adds 18 new reviews every month. At your current pace (4/month), the gap grows every month — you never catch up.

**You don't need to catch Dundee.** What you need is to stop falling behind and close the gap with the practices closer to your size:
- Cary Grove: 384 reviews, 5/month
- Pet Vet: 227 reviews, 5/month

**Target:** 8-10 new Google reviews per month

**Simple system:**
- After every positive appointment, the front desk asks: "Would you mind leaving us a Google review? It really helps other pet owners find us."
- Have a short link ready (Google provides this in your GBP dashboard under "Ask for reviews")
- Text or email the link to the client within an hour of their visit
- Don't incentivize or script the reviews — just make it easy

**Coach clients to mention specifics:** If they mention their city and the service ("Great dental cleaning for Max — we drive from Crystal Lake and it's worth it"), those keywords help your rankings. You can't tell people what to write, but you can say "If you have a moment to mention what brought you in today, that helps other pet owners know what to expect."
