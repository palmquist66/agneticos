## Step 5: Photo Upload — 8 Week Plan (start now) — HIGH impact

HP's last GBP photo upload was **2014**. That's 12 years ago. Google rewards profiles with fresh images, and potential clients notice when a practice looks active vs. dormant.

**Goal:** 3 photos per week for 8 weeks (24 total), then 1 per week ongoing.

**How to upload:**
1. On your management panel, click **"Add photo"** or go to the Photos section

**Specs:** JPG or PNG, at least 720x720 pixels, under 5 MB, real photos only (no stock).

### Response Rules

1. **Respond to every review** — 100% response rate, no exceptions
2. **Respond within 24 hours** — your current avg is 44.6 days. Dundee does it in 2.3. Speed matters for ranking signals.
3. **Always use the reviewer's name** and **pet's name** (if mentioned) — this is already your strength, keep doing it
4. **Mention a specific service** in every response — the service they referenced or came in for
5. **Mention "Lake in the Hills"** (or the reviewer's city) naturally in every response
6. **Rotate variations** — never use the same template twice in a row
7. **For negative reviews:** Never argue publicly. Offer phone number to resolve offline. Keep it 3-5 sentences.
8. **Never share medical details, billing info, or anything private**

### Catching Up the Backlog

Work through unanswered reviews newest to oldest. Replying to old reviews is still better than leaving them blank. Google doesn't penalize late responses — but the ranking signal is strongest in the first 24-48 hours.

**Simple system:**
- After every positive appointment, the front desk asks: "Would you mind leaving us a Google review? It really helps other pet owners find us."
- Have a short link ready (Google provides this in your GBP dashboard under "Ask for reviews")
- Text or email the link within an hour of their visit
- Don't incentivize or script reviews — just make it easy
- Coach clients to mention specifics: city name + service ("Great dental cleaning for Max — we drive from Crystal Lake"). You can't tell people what to write, but you can say "If you have a moment to mention what brought you in today, that helps other pet owners know what to expect."
