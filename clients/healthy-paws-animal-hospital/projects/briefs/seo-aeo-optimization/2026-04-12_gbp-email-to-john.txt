STEP 2: ADD A SECONDARY CATEGORY (5 min)

Right now the profile only says "Animal Hospital." More people search "veterinarian near me" than "animal hospital near me," so adding Veterinarian as a secondary category gets you into more results.

Go to Edit profile > Business category. Below "Animal Hospital," click "Add another category" and type "Veterinarian." Select it and save.

This can take 24-72 hours to go live since Google reviews category changes.

Don't add "Emergency Veterinary Service" unless you actually offer emergency or after-hours care. Google will suspend listings with misleading categories.


STEP 3: COMPLETE YOUR ATTRIBUTES (10-15 min)

These are the badges and details on your listing like wheelchair access, accepts new patients, etc. The more you fill out, the more signals Google has.

Go to Edit profile > More tab. Go through every option and toggle the ones that apply:

- Wheelchair-accessible entrance, restroom, parking
- Accepts new patients (yes)
- Online appointments (if you have online booking)
- Appointment required
- Wi-Fi available
- Parking available
- Any identity attributes that apply (women-owned, veteran-owned, etc.) — these show a badge on mobile results

Check everything that's true. Don't mark anything that isn't.


STEP 4: UPLOAD PHOTOS (start this week, ongoing)

Your last GBP photo upload was 2014. Winding Creek and Pet Vet both have recent photos. Google's algorithm rewards profiles with fresh images, and potential clients notice when a listing looks active vs abandoned.

The plan: 3 photos per week for 8 weeks, then 1 per week after that. Phone photos are fine — natural lighting, in focus, no filters.

On your management panel, click "Add photo" or go to the Photos section. First thing: set your logo and cover photo.

Here's a week-by-week schedule:

Week 1: Front of building with signage, reception area, full team group photo
Week 2: Parking lot and entrance, an exam room, Dr. Karen with a patient
Week 3: Waiting area from a different angle, treatment area, a tech at work
Week 4: Another exterior angle, surgery or dental suite, vet examining a dog
Week 5: Seasonal exterior, lab area, vet examining a cat
Week 6: Close-up of signage, X-ray or diagnostic equipment, happy pet at checkout
Week 7: Interior detail (artwork, calming features), team member with a pet, action shot like a vaccination or wellness exam
Week 8: Fresh exterior shot, any new equipment, candid team moment

Tip: pick one person to be the "photo person" each week. Kadee might be good for this. Consistency matters more than perfection. Get verbal OK from pet owners before posting photos of their animals.

Short videos work too — a 30-second facility walkthrough or team intro.


STEP 5: RESPOND TO EVERY REVIEW (1-3 hrs to catch up, then 5 min/day)

You have 158 reviews at 4.7 stars, which is solid. The issue is response rate — you're around 30-40%. Winding Creek responds to 100% of theirs. Potential patients notice that, and Google uses response rate as a ranking signal.

On your management panel, click "Read reviews." Find reviews without a reply and click "Reply."

For positive reviews (4-5 stars), keep it 2-3 sentences. Thank them by name, mention something specific from their review (especially the pet's name if they included it), invite them back:

"Thank you, Sarah! We're so glad Max's dental cleaning went well and that our team made you both feel comfortable. We look forward to seeing you at his next visit!"

"Thanks for the kind words, Mike! Buddy is such a great patient. We're happy the new puppy wellness plan has been helpful."

For negative reviews (1-2 stars), don't get defensive. Don't share any private pet or medical details publicly. Take it offline:

"Thank you for sharing your feedback. We're sorry your experience didn't meet expectations and take all concerns seriously. Please reach out to us at (847) 854-2322 so we can discuss this directly."

Rules going forward:
- Respond within 24 hours of new reviews
- Vary your responses (don't copy-paste the same thing every time)
- Never argue publicly
- Keep it 2-5 sentences
- Mention the pet's name when the reviewer includes it

To catch up on the backlog, work through them oldest to newest. Replying to a year-old review is still better than leaving it blank.

Google is also testing AI-suggested replies. If you see a suggestion button next to a review, it'll draft something you can edit before posting. Always personalize it before submitting.
