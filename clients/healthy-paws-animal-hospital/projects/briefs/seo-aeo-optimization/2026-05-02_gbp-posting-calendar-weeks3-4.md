POST 8: Pet Care Tip, Senior Pet Wellness
(Publish Wednesday or Thursday)

Is your dog or cat over seven years old? Their health needs are changing in ways that aren't always obvious from the outside.

Senior pets are more prone to arthritis, dental disease, kidney issues, and changes in thyroid function. The tricky part is that dogs and cats are good at hiding discomfort. By the time you notice something's off, the problem may have been developing for months.

We recommend twice-yearly wellness exams for senior pets. More frequent check-ins let us track trends in bloodwork and weight, catching shifts early when they're easier to manage. Early detection makes all the difference in keeping senior pets comfortable and active.

If your older dog or cat hasn't been in recently, give us a call. We'll take a thorough look and walk you through everything we find.

POST 10: Service Spotlight, Vaccinations
(Publish Monday or Tuesday)

Keeping your pet's vaccinations current is one of the simplest things you can do to protect their health. But not every pet needs the same vaccines on the same schedule.

At Healthy Paws, we build vaccination plans around your pet's age, breed, lifestyle, and risk factors. A dog who spends time at the dog park has different needs than a cat who never goes outside. We take the time to explain what each vaccine does and why we're recommending it, so you're making informed decisions, not just checking boxes.

If you're not sure whether your dog or cat is up to date, bring them in. We'll review their history and put together a plan that makes sense for their situation.

Call (815) 322-5400 to schedule.

POST 11: Pet Care Tip, Nutrition
(Publish Wednesday or Thursday)

What you feed your dog or cat shows up everywhere — their energy, their coat, their digestion, how they carry weight, even how they age.

The problem is there are hundreds of pet food brands making claims, and it's hard to know what's real versus what's just good packaging. Our team can help you sort through it. We offer nutrition guidance based on your pet's breed, age, weight, and any health conditions they're managing.

Good nutrition is one of the best things you can do for your pet long-term. And it doesn't have to be complicated. Sometimes it's as simple as switching to a food that actually fits your pet's needs.

Ask us about nutrition at your next visit, or give us a call if you have questions now.

Here's something most people don't expect when they visit Healthy Paws for the first time. There's almost no time in the waiting room.

It's a small thing, but it changes how the whole visit feels — for you and your pet.
