## What's Working (Without Website Changes)

1. **GBP posting activity is helping.** Two service queries (spay/neuter, dental) flipped from invisible to visible. The posting work signals to Google that HPAH is active, which seems to be boosting the existing pages' authority.

2. **HPAH's about page is doing heavy lifting.** It mentions Crystal Lake, Huntley, Lakewood, and Algonquin as service areas. This is why HPAH surfaces on some broader queries. But a single about page can't compete with dedicated location pages.

3. **Brand search is solid.** Anyone searching HPAH by name gets a strong, multi-source result (website, Yelp, Google, BBB, GeniusVets, PetInsuranceReview, Facebook).

---

## What's Still Broken

1. **Zero visibility in Crystal Lake, Huntley, and Algonquin for general "best vet" queries.** This won't change without dedicated location pages. Pet Vet proves the model works -- they have pages and they show up.

2. **Fear Free still unclaimed.** HPAH's site doesn't use the words "Fear Free" on any page. Their approach IS fear-free, but AI models can't cite what doesn't exist. Pet Vet's "Fear Free trained" label appears in Huntley results -- that's a searchable credential.

3. **No dedicated service pages.** Competitors with pages for dental care, senior care, and spay/neuter show up for those queries with detailed descriptions. HPAH has one services list page. AI models quote the competitor with the dedicated page because there's more to cite.

4. **No structured data.** Still zero JSON-LD schema. AI models that parse structured data (Google AI Overviews especially) have nothing to work with.

5. **No FAQ content.** Still zero Q&A formatted content on the website.

6. **Reddit: zero.** Same as April.
