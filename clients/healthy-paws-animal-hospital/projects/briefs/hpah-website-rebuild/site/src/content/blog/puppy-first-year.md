## Congratulations on Your New Puppy

Bringing home a new puppy is one of life's great joys — and it comes with a lot of questions. Our team at Healthy Paws Animal Hospital has guided thousands of puppies through their first year, and we have put together this timeline so you know exactly what to expect and when.

The first year of your puppy's life is when we lay the foundation for a lifetime of good health. Vaccinations, parasite prevention, nutrition, behavior, and developmental milestones all happen in rapid succession. Having a clear roadmap helps you feel confident that your puppy is getting everything they need.

## 6-8 Weeks: First Veterinary Visit

This is typically when puppies come home and have their first visit with us. During this appointment, we:

- Perform a comprehensive physical exam (heart, lungs, eyes, ears, joints, abdomen)
- Start the **DHPP vaccination series** (distemper, hepatitis, parainfluenza, parvovirus)
- Begin **intestinal parasite screening** and deworming
- Discuss nutrition and recommend an appropriate puppy food for their breed size
- Start **heartworm and flea/tick prevention**
- Talk about crate training, potty training, and early socialization
- Address any concerns you have about your new family member

We take extra time at puppy visits because we know you have questions. We want you to feel fully informed and supported.

## 10-12 Weeks: Second Round

At this visit, your puppy receives:

- **DHPP booster** (second in the series)
- **Bordetella vaccine** (kennel cough) if your puppy will attend daycare, training classes, or boarding
- **Leptospirosis vaccine** (first dose) — important in our area due to wildlife exposure
- Continued parasite prevention
- Weight check and growth assessment
- Discussion of **socialization milestones** — this is the critical socialization window

### About the Socialization Window

Between 3 and 16 weeks, puppies are in their primary socialization period. Positive experiences during this time shape their behavior for life. We encourage:

- Meeting different people (varying ages, appearances, voices)
- Exposure to different surfaces, sounds, and environments
- Positive interactions with other vaccinated dogs
- Short car rides, gentle handling of paws and ears, and other daily-life experiences

We balance socialization with safety — until your puppy is fully vaccinated, avoid dog parks, pet stores, and areas with unknown dogs.

## 14-16 Weeks: Final Puppy Boosters

This visit is an important milestone:

- **Final DHPP booster** — your puppy is now building strong immunity
- **Rabies vaccine** — required by Illinois law
- **Leptospirosis booster** (second dose)
- Microchip implantation (we strongly recommend this)
- Discussion of **spay or neuter timing** based on your puppy's breed and size
- Behavioral check-in — we address any emerging concerns

After this visit, your puppy's core vaccination series is complete. You can now safely explore dog parks, trails, and social environments.

## 5-6 Months: Spay or Neuter Consultation

The timing of spay or neuter surgery is something we discuss individually based on:

- Your puppy's breed (large breeds may benefit from waiting longer)
- Their growth rate and development
- Your family's situation and lifestyle
- Current veterinary research on optimal timing

We do not take a one-size-fits-all approach. We will discuss the pros and cons of different timing and help you make the decision that is right for your specific puppy.

## 6-12 Months: Adolescence

During this period, your puppy is growing rapidly and going through behavioral adolescence. We recommend:

- **Continued monthly parasite prevention** year-round
- Transition from puppy food to adult food (timing varies by breed size)
- Addressing any **behavioral changes** that emerge during adolescence
- Dental check — adult teeth should be fully in by 6-7 months
- Monitoring growth and body condition

## 12 Months: First Adult Wellness Exam

At one year, your puppy graduates to annual wellness care:

- Comprehensive adult physical exam
- **DHPP annual booster** (then moves to every 3 years)
- **Rabies booster** (then every 3 years per Illinois law)
- Annual heartworm test
- Baseline adult bloodwork (recommended)
- Updated nutrition recommendations for their adult needs

## Tips for a Great First Year

Based on our experience with thousands of puppies, here is what we recommend:

- **Be consistent** with training and boundaries from day one
- **Puppy-proof your home** before they arrive (electrical cords, toxic plants, small objects)
- **Invest in quality nutrition** — it makes a noticeable difference in coat, energy, and development
- **Start dental care early** — get your puppy used to having their mouth handled and teeth brushed
- **Keep all follow-up appointments** — the vaccination schedule works because of proper timing and boosters
