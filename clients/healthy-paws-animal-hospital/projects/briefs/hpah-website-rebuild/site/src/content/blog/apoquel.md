## What Is Apoquel?

Apoquel (oclacitinib) is a prescription medication we commonly recommend for dogs suffering from allergic itch. Unlike older treatments such as steroids or antihistamines, Apoquel targets specific pathways in the immune system that trigger itching and inflammation — without broadly suppressing your dog's entire immune response.

Since its introduction, Apoquel has become one of the most frequently prescribed medications in veterinary dermatology. Our team at Healthy Paws Animal Hospital uses it as part of a comprehensive approach to managing your dog's allergies.

## How Apoquel Works

Your dog's itching starts at the cellular level. When allergens trigger a reaction, certain enzymes called Janus kinases (JAK) send signals that produce the itch sensation and inflammatory response. Apoquel selectively inhibits these JAK enzymes, particularly JAK1, to interrupt the itch cycle.

Most dogs experience noticeable relief within 4 hours of the first dose, with the full effect visible within 24 hours. This rapid response is one of the reasons we recommend it for dogs who are miserable from constant scratching, licking, and chewing.

## When We Recommend Apoquel

We typically consider Apoquel for dogs who:

- Have been diagnosed with allergic dermatitis (environmental allergies)
- Experience chronic itching that affects their quality of life
- Haven't responded adequately to other treatments
- Need long-term itch management beyond short steroid courses

Apoquel is approved for dogs 12 months and older. It can be used for both short-term flare-ups and long-term maintenance, depending on your pet's specific situation.

## Important Considerations

While Apoquel is generally well-tolerated, our team believes you should be informed about a few things:

**Monitoring matters.** We recommend bloodwork before starting Apoquel and periodically during treatment. This helps us catch any changes early and ensure the medication continues to be a safe choice for your dog.

**It manages, not cures.** Apoquel controls symptoms but does not eliminate the underlying allergy. We often combine it with other strategies — such as medicated baths, fatty acid supplements, or allergy testing — to address the root cause.

**Potential side effects** are uncommon but can include decreased appetite, vomiting, or diarrhea, particularly in the first few days. More rarely, some dogs may be more susceptible to infections or develop skin lumps that should be evaluated.
