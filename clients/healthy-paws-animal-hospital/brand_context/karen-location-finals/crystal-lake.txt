Supporting Crystal Lake Pet Owners

Fighting Fear

When choosing a veterinarian, while credentials and location matter, equally important is how your pet and you feel before, during, and after a visit. It does not matter how many titles a doctor has, or how fancy their equipment, if they cannot effectively explain options to you as an owner, take the time to answer your questions, or support you when you are most worried for your pet. If your pet is terrified and the veterinary team is not able to help them in a supportive way their health is put at risk.  Nervous pets often lead to preoccupied owners limiting their ability to absorb the information being presented in the exam room.  This is one of the reasons why we liken a trip to Healthy Paws to a trip to grandma’s house, lots of treats and positive attention to help calm everyone’s nerves!

Spending time with families and using a variety of calming techniques for our patients sets us up for success. From care coordinators taking clients directly to an exam room, providing multiple forms of treats (liver treats, peanut butter, whipped cream, string cheese, and more!), veterinary technicians asking detailed questions, and our veterinarians explaining how to best help your pet — in this hospital, care is a team effort.

Options for Crystal Lake Pet Owners

I love learning, I love teaching, I love veterinary medicine. The combination of all three is just plain fun. We do not expect clients to understand everything that is happening with their pets, that is our job. Our goal is for owners to leave our practice with a better grasp of their pet's health than when they arrived, knowing they have choices, support, and resources. We explain our physical exam findings in real time, provide options and discuss pros and cons, and we let owners know what to watch for if things are not going as planned. We believe that our team and clients are empowered by learning and strive to incorporate it into everything we do.

What is your approach to fearful or anxious pets?

Often we ask owners to visit us with their pet prior to their first visit; it gives us the chance to create a favorable first impression prior to them coming in for their first official visit. Every member of our team is trained in gentle, low-stress handling. We use pheromone diffusers, calming techniques, and a pace that lets your pet settle before the exam begins. Many clients tell us their once-anxious pets seem noticeably calmer on their second visit.

Community for Crystal Lake Pet Owners

Dog Friendly Finds

Looking for some fun places to take your dog in Crystal Lake?  Here are a few suggestions:

Hound Town Dog Park — A 3-acre, fully fenced, off-leash dog park inside Lippold Park Sport Complex. Requires registration, vaccination records, and a keycard. Lippold is also a great spot for on-leash walks with your pup.

Veterans Acres Park — Wooded hiking trails where leashed dogs are welcome. Beautiful for a nature walk. Don't forget to check for ticks and use preventative — Veterans Acres is an amazing trek, but not without potential tick exposure.

Dog-Friendly Dining & Drinks

Crystal Lake Brewing — Dogs are welcome on the patio. They keep dog mats and cookies on hand. Genuinely dog-friendly, not just tolerant. Bring something for the humans to share with each other also.

The Cottage — Seasonal dog-friendly patio with pub fare, burgers, tacos, and more. A local favorite.

Cafe Olympic — Outdoor sidewalk seating and a dedicated dog menu. Yes, really.

Julie Ann's Frozen Custard — Seasonal spot where you and your dog can both get a treat. Every day brings a new flavor.

Starbucks (Northwest Highway) — Puppuccinos available on request. Outdoor, pet-friendly tables.

Cats About Town

While cats may not get to visit a dog park or patio restaurant, they have their own local resources. An important reminder for cat owners: make sure your pet's microchip information is current. If your feline decides to take a walkabout in Crystal Lake, an updated chip is the fastest way back home.

Thomas' Tails — A family-owned natural pet food store in the heart of historic downtown Crystal Lake. Quality food, treats, toys, and accessories for cats — and three resident cats in the store. A neighborhood treasure worth visiting. (31 E Crystal Lake Ave)

Thinking of Adding a Cat to Your Family?

Crystal Lake has wonderful adoption resources close to home.

McHenry County Animal Control & Adoption Center — The county's official adoption center, located right in Crystal Lake. Cats are spayed or neutered, vaccinated, and microchipped before adoption.
