# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Casual sports bettors, intermediate skill level. They already have DraftKings and FanDuel on their phone, place a few prop bets a week, and are not trying to go pro. They want to lose less, not grind spreadsheets. Their current "research" is a gut feeling plus whatever ESPN says. They open the site on their phone between games or at a bar with friends. They are skeptical of anything that sounds like a picks service.

## Product Purpose

BetBro is a prop research tool that pulls lines, averages, injury reports, and matchup data into a plain-English verdict in under 10 seconds. The site's job is to convert skeptical bettors during free beta into active users. It must communicate three things fast: what BetBro does, why it's different (it tells you when NOT to bet), and that it's free right now.

## Brand Personality

Direct. Punchy. Honest. BetBro talks like the sharpest friend in your group chat who actually does the homework. Not a tout, not a guru, not a hype machine. Short sentences. Fragments on purpose. PG-13 casual. The defining trait: BetBro will tell you to walk away from a bad bet, and that's the whole point.

Three words: honest, fast, unflinching.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient hero blobs, icon-feature grids, "trusted by 10,000+ users" social proof bars, stock photography of people high-fiving. BetBro is not a B2B tool.
- Picks services and tout pages: flashing neon, "VIP access", countdown timers, guaranteed-lock energy. BetBro is the opposite of this.
- Over-designed sports media: ESPN's cluttered dashboards, Yahoo Sports' ad-heavy layouts. Too much noise.
- Dark-mode-for-dark-mode's-sake crypto/fintech sites: neon gradients on black, glowing orbs, abstract 3D renders that mean nothing.

## Design Principles

1. **Say it once, say it fast.** Every section earns its scroll. No restated headings, no filler paragraphs, no "but wait, there's more" padding. If a section doesn't add new information, cut it.
2. **Show the product, not the promise.** Real UI, real data, real verdicts. Screenshots and examples over abstract illustrations. The product is the proof.
3. **Honest confidence.** The site should feel like someone who knows what they're talking about and doesn't need to prove it with visual noise. Restraint is the flex.
4. **Night-game energy.** Dark, high-contrast, emerald cutting through. The visual register of checking your phone during a game, not browsing a SaaS marketing page at your desk.
5. **Friction-free conversion.** Every CTA is obvious, every path to signup is short. No forms, no email gates, no "schedule a demo." Try it free, right now.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard best practices. Sufficient contrast ratios on dark backgrounds (especially emerald-on-dark). Keyboard navigable. Screen-reader-friendly structure. No motion-dependent interactions for critical content.
