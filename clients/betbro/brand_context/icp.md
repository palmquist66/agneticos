# BetBro — Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are

**In one sentence:** Casual sports bettors who already have a sportsbook app open and want a quick gut check on a prop before pulling the trigger.

**Situation:** Already betting — has DraftKings, FanDuel, or similar installed. Places a few bets a week, mostly props. Not trying to go pro, just wants to lose less and win more.
**Identity:** "I'm a sports fan who bets for fun." Not a sharp, not a degen — somewhere in the middle. Follows their teams, watches the games, likes having skin in the game.
**Role:** Has a full-time job that isn't sports betting. This is entertainment with a side of wanting to be smart about it.
**Stage:** Intermediate — knows what props are, has placed plenty, but doesn't have a research process. Goes on feel and whatever the app suggests.

## Primary Pain

**The problem:** They're placing props based on gut feeling and whatever line the sportsbook pushes in front of them — and they know they're leaving money on the table.
**What it costs them:** Lost bets that could've been avoided, the nagging feeling of "I should've checked," and the slow bleed of betting volume without an edge.
**How they describe it:** "I just wish I had a quick way to check if this is actually a good bet." / "I don't have time to research every prop." / "Half the time I'm just guessing."

**Secondary pains:**
- Information overload — stat sites exist but they're built for sharps, not casual bettors
- Betting too many props — no signal telling them when to sit one out
- Post-bet regret — finding out the info was there but they didn't look

## What They've Tried

| Alternative | Why it didn't work | What they learned |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Gut feeling / eye test | Hit-or-miss, no consistency | They need some data, just not too much |
| Checking stats on ESPN/sportsbook | Surface-level, doesn't factor matchups or recent form | Raw stats without context aren't enough |
| Picks services / touts on Twitter | Expensive, no transparency, felt like gambling on someone else's opinion | They want to make their own call with better info |
| Detailed analytics tools (OddsJam, etc.) | Too complex, too many features, built for sharps | They want the answer, not a dashboard |

**Sophistication level:** Intermediate — they've tried a couple of things, but nothing stuck because everything is either too simple (gut) or too complex (sharp tools).

## Desired Outcome

**What "after" looks like:** Open BetBro alongside their sportsbook app, get a clear read on a prop in 10 seconds, and either bet with confidence or walk away knowing they dodged a coinflip.
**What they'd tell a friend:** "Dude, this app just tells you straight up — bet it or skip it. Takes like 10 seconds."
**The deeper motivation:** Feeling smart about their bets. Not getting played by the sportsbook. Being the friend in the group chat who makes sharp calls without being a nerd about it.

## Objections

| Objection | What's really behind it | How to address |
|-----------|------------------------|----------------|
| "Why would I pay for betting info?" | They've been burned by paid picks services | It's not picks — it's research. And it's free during beta. $7.99/month is one bad prop bet. |
| "I don't trust AI for betting" | Skepticism about black-box predictions | BetBro doesn't predict — it pulls real data and explains it in English. You make the call. |
| "I already check the stats" | They think they're doing enough research | BetBro factors matchups, injury reports, and recent form together — not just one stat line. In 10 seconds. |
| "Another betting app?" | App fatigue | It works alongside your existing app — not replacing anything. Open it, check the prop, close it. |

## Their Language

**Words they use for the problem:**
- "Is this a good bet?" — the core question they're trying to answer
- "I'm just guessing" — admitting they have no process
- "I don't have time to research" — the time constraint

**Words they use for the desired outcome:**
- "Quick check" — speed is everything
- "Tell me if it's worth it" — they want a verdict, not data
- "Bet smarter" — aspirational but grounded

**Words that would turn them off:**
- "Proprietary algorithm" — sounds like a scam
- "Guaranteed winners" — immediate credibility loss
- "Advanced analytics platform" — not for them, too serious
- "Optimize your portfolio" — they're not running a hedge fund

**Jargon level:** Light — knows props, lines, overs/unders, parlays, L5/L10. Doesn't know or care about Kelly criterion, CLV, or steam moves.

## Where They Hang Out

| Platform/Channel | How they use it | Why it matters |
|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| X/Twitter | Follows sports accounts, betting personalities, checks lines | Best channel for hot takes and engagement |
| Instagram | Follows sports highlights, meme accounts | Visual content, brand awareness |
| Reddit (r/sportsbook, r/sportsbetting) | Lurks for tips, reads recaps, occasionally posts | Authenticity matters here — no self-promo |
| YouTube | Watches betting breakdowns, player highlights | Long-form trust building |
| Group chats (iMessage, Discord) | Shares bets with friends, talks trash | Word of mouth — "check out this app" |

**People they trust:** Friends who bet, sports podcasters (Pat McAfee, Pardon My Take types), Twitter sharps who show receipts — not paid touts.

## Buying Triggers

What pushes them from "considering" to "downloading":
- A bad beat they could've avoided — "if I'd just checked the matchup data..."
- Seeing a friend use it and make a smart call
- Free trial / beta access — zero risk to try
- March Madness / NFL season start — betting activity spikes and so does the desire for an edge
- A viral tweet or post showing BetBro calling a walk-away that saved someone money

## Secondary Segments

### Segment: DFS Players
- **Who:** Daily fantasy players (PrizePicks, Underdog) who need player prop context for lineup building
- **Key difference from primary:** More volume-oriented, may want to research 5-10 props at once, slightly more analytical
- **Priority:** Medium — natural adjacent market, same data needs

### Segment: Sports Content Creators
- **Who:** Twitter/YouTube personalities who post betting content and need quick research to back their takes
- **Key difference from primary:** Using BetBro as a content tool, not just personal betting
- **Priority:** Low — small segment but high word-of-mouth potential
