### 2. DK Replay: From Launch to Backlash in 6 Days

DraftKings launched "DK Replay" in Oregon on March 25 -- a product that lets you bet pitch-by-pitch on anonymized historical MLB at-bats, 24/7. Six days later, it's already being adjusted after regulatory pressure.

**What it is:**
- Bet on whether the next pitch is a ball, strike, or in-play
- Players are anonymized but rated bronze/silver/gold based on real stats
- Operates on a pitch clock timer -- you must bet before the clock expires
- Uses hundreds of thousands of historical plate appearances
- Available 24/7 -- not tied to live games

**The backlash:**
- GamblingHarm.org calls it "akin to a slot machine" dressed up as sports betting
- Oregon State Lottery received complaints about the countdown timer increasing addiction risk
- DraftKings and Oregon Lottery "collectively decided to adjust DK Replay to more closely resemble the pace of a live baseball game" and add responsible gaming prompts
- LegalSportsBetting.com ran "Is DraftKings New DK Replay Predatory?" -- the answer was basically yes
- Sportico headline: "Oregon Insists DraftKings' Historic Pitch Product Isn't Casino Game"
- Multiple states (Massachusetts, New Jersey, Louisiana) already considering microbetting bans

**The lawsuit (filed March 24):**
- Public Health Advocacy Institute sued DraftKings, FanDuel, NFL, and Genius Sports in Philadelphia
- Alleges microbetting products are "unreasonably dangerous products intentionally designed to maximize addiction"
- Plaintiff 1: Lost $170,000+ across DraftKings and FanDuel
- Plaintiff 2: Lost ~$1.86 MILLION and was hospitalized for suicidal ideation
- Alleges VIP hosts feigned friendships to encourage continued betting
- Called gambling "as addictive as tobacco, heroin, alcohol, and cocaine"

**BetBro angle:** This is a HUGE content moment. BetBro's entire positioning is "we tell you when to walk away." DK Replay is the exact opposite -- it's designed to keep you betting 24/7 on randomized outcomes with no off switch. Content angle: "DraftKings just built a product that never wants you to stop. We built one that tells you when to." This is the kind of news story that validates BetBro's whole thesis.
