# GLP-1 Companion — Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are

**In one sentence:** Adults on GLP-1 medications (or about to start) who are actively trying to manage their health data but are overwhelmed by the number of disconnected apps and tools involved.

**Situation:** They've been prescribed a GLP-1 (semaglutide, tirzepatide, or similar) for weight management or metabolic health. They're motivated — they didn't take starting medication lightly. But now they're drowning in fragmented tracking: weight in one app, food in another, side effects in notes, dosing schedules in calendar reminders, and lab results in a patient portal they forget to check.

**Identity:** They see themselves as someone taking their health seriously. They're not "on a diet" — they're managing a medical treatment. Many are private about being on a GLP-1 due to stigma. They want to feel informed and in control, not overwhelmed.

**Role:** Self-managing their GLP-1 journey day-to-day. Their doctor sees them every few months, but between appointments they're on their own making sense of what's happening.

**Stage:** Two primary segments — newcomers (first 0-3 months, still figuring out what to track and how) and established users (3+ months, have a routine but can't see the big picture across their scattered data).

## Primary Pain

**The problem:** "I'm tracking everything but I can't see how it all connects. My weight, my food, my side effects, my energy — it's all in different places and none of it talks to each other."

**What it costs them:** Time toggling between apps daily. Anxiety from not understanding why their body is doing what it's doing. Missed patterns that could help them (or their doctor) make better decisions. Feeling like they're flying blind between appointments.

**How they describe it:** "I have data everywhere but insight nowhere." / "I don't know if this is normal or if something's wrong." / "I wish I could just show my doctor everything in one screen."

**Secondary pains:**
- Side effect anxiety — "Is this nausea normal at this dose? How long did it last for other people?"
- Dosing confusion — tracking titration schedules, what happened at each dose level
- Isolation — nobody in their immediate circle is on a GLP-1, online communities are overwhelming and full of noise

## What They've Tried

| Alternative | Why it didn't work | What they learned |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| MyFitnessPal / Lose It | Food tracking only — no GLP-1 context, no side effect logging, no dosing | "It's built for dieters, not people on medication" |
| Apple Health / Google Fit | Aggregates some data but no GLP-1 specific features, no pattern surfacing | "It collects data but doesn't help me understand it" |
| Spreadsheets / Notes app | Manual, tedious, hard to visualize, easy to fall behind | "I kept it up for two weeks then stopped" |
| GLP-1 Facebook groups / Reddit | Information overload, anecdotal, hard to find signal in the noise | "Everyone's experience is different and it's scary" |
| Noom / coaching apps | Expensive, generic weight loss framing, not GLP-1 aware | "They don't understand that this isn't just a diet" |

**Sophistication level:** Intermediate — they've tried 2-3 tracking approaches and been frustrated by all of them. They know what they want (connected data, clear patterns) but haven't found a tool that delivers it.

## Desired Outcome

**What "after" looks like:** They open one app and see everything — weight trend, food log, side effect timeline, dosing history, energy patterns — on one screen. They spot a pattern ("my nausea peaks 2 days after dose increases and resolves by day 5") and feel informed instead of anxious. They walk into their doctor's appointment with a clear picture to share.

**What they'd tell a friend:** "I finally stopped guessing. I can actually see what's going on — like, my energy dip every Tuesday makes sense now because that's two days after my injection. It's all in one place."

**The deeper motivation:** Control and confidence in a situation that feels uncertain. GLP-1s are still stigmatized and poorly understood by friends/family. Having clear data gives them agency over their own health story — and proof (for themselves and their doctor) that they're doing this thoughtfully.

## Objections

| Objection | What's really behind it | How to address |
|-----------|------------------------|----------------|
| "I don't want another app to maintain" | Tracking fatigue — they've been burned by apps that demand daily effort | Show that this replaces multiple apps, not adds to them. "One app instead of four" |
| "Is my health data safe?" | GLP-1 stigma + general health data privacy concern | Clear, upfront privacy policy. No selling data. HIPAA-aware if applicable. |
| "Will this actually help or is it just more tracking?" | Skepticism from tools that collect data but never surface insight | Lead with the pattern-surfacing — "We don't just store your data, we show you what it means" |
| "I'm not tech-savvy enough for this" | Fear of complex dashboards and settings | Clean, guided onboarding. Show the value in the first 60 seconds. |

## Their Language

**Words they use for the problem:**
- "I'm tracking everything in different places" — describes the fragmentation
- "I don't know if this is normal" — side effect anxiety
- "My doctor only sees me every 3 months" — the between-appointments gap
- "I feel like I'm doing this alone" — isolation of the GLP-1 journey

**Words they use for the desired outcome:**
- "I want to see the big picture"
- "I just want everything in one place"
- "I want to know what's working and what's not"

**Words that would turn them off:**
- "Weight loss hack" — trivializes their medical journey
- "Biohacking" — wrong subculture, feels bro-science
- "Transform your body" — reductive, triggers diet culture sensitivity
- "Patient portal" — too clinical, they get enough of that from their provider

**Jargon level:** Light to moderate — they know GLP-1 terms (titration, semaglutide, tirzepatide, Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound) but may not know compounding, half-life, or pharmacokinetic details. Explain anything beyond basic medication names.

## Buying Triggers

What pushes them from "considering" to "downloading":
- Starting or changing their GLP-1 dose — the moment tracking becomes urgent
- A frustrating doctor's appointment where they couldn't explain what happened between visits
- Seeing a post from someone whose GLP-1 app surfaced a pattern they missed
- Hitting a plateau and wanting data to understand why
- A friend or online community member recommending it specifically

## Secondary Segments

### Segment: Type 2 Diabetics on GLP-1s
- **Who:** People prescribed GLP-1s primarily for blood sugar management, not weight loss
- **Key difference from primary:** Additional data needs (blood glucose, A1C tracking), different relationship with the medication (prescribed for diabetes management, weight loss is secondary benefit)
- **Priority:** Medium — larger market but more competitors (MySugr, Glucose Buddy, etc.)

### Segment: Healthcare Providers
- **Who:** Endocrinologists, obesity medicine doctors, PCPs prescribing GLP-1s
- **Key difference from primary:** They want patient data summaries, population-level patterns, compliance insights — not personal tracking
- **Priority:** Low for now — B2B play for later. Focus on consumer first.

### Segment: GLP-1 Curious
- **Who:** People researching GLP-1s before getting a prescription
- **Key difference from primary:** Not yet on medication, in research/decision mode. Need education more than tracking
- **Priority:** Medium — content marketing audience that converts to users once they start medication
