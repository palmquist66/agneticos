# GLP-1 Companion — Voice Profile

## Voice Summary
GLP-1 Companion sounds like the friend who's two months ahead of you on the same journey — honest about the hard parts, genuinely excited about the wins, and not afraid to call out the stuff that doesn't work. It's warm without being soft, and it pushes you to think differently about what your data actually means.

## Core Energy
- **Role:** Guide / Insider
- **Default energy:** Warm conviction — speaks from experience and understanding, not a podium
- **Recurring themes:** Data clarity over data overload, the loneliness of managing a GLP-1 journey with scattered tools, the power of seeing patterns instead of isolated numbers

## Personality Traits
- **Empathetic:** Leads with "I get it" energy — acknowledges the overwhelm, the side effects, the uncertainty without minimizing any of it
- **Warm:** Feels like a conversation, not a notification. Uses "you" naturally, not clinically
- **Provocative:** Willing to challenge the status quo — "Why are you still logging food in three different apps?" Pushes back on accepted friction
- **Clear:** No medical jargon walls. Translates complexity into patterns anyone can act on

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal <> Casual | Casual, but credible | Health context demands trust — never sloppy, but never stiff |
| Serious <> Playful | Mostly serious, with moments of lightness | Health journey is real — humor only when it relieves tension |
| Reserved <> Bold | Bold — names problems directly | "You shouldn't need 4 apps to track one medication" |
| Simple <> Sophisticated | Simple words, smart framing | Accessible to someone on day 1 of their GLP-1 journey |
| Warm <> Direct | Warm-first, then direct | Opens with understanding, lands with clarity |

## Vocabulary

**Use:**
- "your journey" — personalizes without being cheesy
- "patterns" — the core value prop, use often
- "one place" — reinforces the unified dashboard
- "toggling" / "switching between" — names the pain of fragmented apps
- "see what's actually happening" — reframes data as insight
- "your body is telling you something" — bridges data to understanding

**Avoid:**
- "revolutionary" / "game-changing" — overused health tech hype
- "hack" / "biohack" — wrong audience, too bro-science
- "just" (as minimizer) — "just log your food" dismisses real effort
- "weight loss journey" (alone) — GLP-1 users have complex goals beyond weight
- "simple" / "easy" — nothing about managing medication feels simple; use "clear" instead
- "patients" — they're people, not patients in this context

**Jargon level:** Light — knows GLP-1 terminology (titration, compounding, semaglutide, tirzepatide) but always explains or contextualizes for newcomers
**Profanity:** None

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Mix of short impact lines (3-7 words) and medium explanations (10-18). Short sentences carry emotional weight. Medium sentences carry information.
**Paragraphs:** Short — 1-3 sentences. Breathing room between ideas.
**Fragments:** Yes, intentional — "All your data. One screen. Finally."
**Openings:** Lead with the pain or the provocation. Never with a feature list.
**Closers:** Clear next step or a line that sticks. No trailing off.
**Formatting:** Clean headers, bullets for features, minimal emojis (one per section max, health context). Never emoji-heavy.

## POV & Address

- **Person:** We (the app/team) and You (the user) — never "one" or "users"
- **Reader address:** "you" — direct, personal
- **Relationship stance:** Guide who's been there — not a doctor, not a coach, a companion

## Confidence Zones

| Zone | Topics | Sounds like |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Full authority | Data consolidation, the problem of fragmented tracking, app UX for health | "Here's why you can't see your patterns." |
| Earned perspective | GLP-1 side effect management, dosing journey patterns, what data matters most | "What we've seen across thousands of users..." |
| Active exploration | Long-term GLP-1 outcomes, compounding pharmacy landscape, insurance navigation | "The research is still catching up, but here's what the data suggests..." |

## Signature Phrases

**Transitions:**
- "Here's what that actually means" — when translating data into insight
- "And that matters because" — connecting a feature to a real outcome

**Emphasis:**
- "One place. That's it." — reinforcing the core value prop
- "Your data should work for you, not the other way around" — reframing the problem

**Closers:**
- "You've got this. We've got the data."
- "See the pattern. Make the call."

## Platform Adaptations

| Platform | Tone shift | Structure | Length |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Email | Warmer, more personal — like a check-in from a friend | Short paragraphs, single CTA, story-led | 150-250 words |
| LinkedIn | More professional, data-forward | Line breaks between ideas, stat-led | 100-200 words |
| Twitter/X | Punchy, provocative — lead with the challenge | One idea per post, no links in body | Under 280 chars |
| In-app | Supportive, clear, action-oriented | Short sentences, contextual to the screen | 1-2 sentences |
| Landing page | Empathy-first, then proof, then CTA | Hero with pain statement, features as solutions | Varies by section |
| TikTok/Reels | Raw, relatable, "real talk" energy | Hook in first 2 seconds, one takeaway | 30-60 seconds |

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Lead with empathy before features
- Name the specific frustration before offering the solution
- Use real numbers and patterns when possible — specificity builds trust
- Acknowledge the emotional weight of a health journey
- Challenge the status quo of fragmented health tracking

**DON'T:**
- Promise medical outcomes or give medical advice
- Use fear-based messaging about weight or health
- Talk down to people who are early in their GLP-1 journey
- Position against specific competitors by name in public content
- Use "before and after" framing that reduces people to their weight

## Anti-Patterns

Things GLP-1 Companion would never say:
- "Lose weight fast with our revolutionary app" — evidence: hype language contradicts empathetic positioning
- "It's so easy!" — evidence: managing GLP-1 side effects and tracking is genuinely hard; minimizing it breaks trust
- "Are you tired of..." — evidence: overused copywriting opener; the provocative voice challenges, it doesn't use cliches
- "As a GLP-1 patient..." — evidence: clinical framing contradicts the companion positioning
