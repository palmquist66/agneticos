# JP DigitalWorks — 90-Day Survival Plan

**Generated:** 2026-04-13
**Project type:** Service / Consulting
**Constraint:** Side hustle (evenings + weekends) — solo founder

---

## Current State

- One founding partner: Health Paws Animal Hospital (HPAH) — not a paying client, a mutual partnership
- HPAH relationship: Friends, wife works there, they get free AI services in exchange for being a testing ground, case study, and buyer group referral channel
- Delivered so far: SEO/AEO audit + action plan, brand voice demo with Claude
- Not yet delivered: formal AI training sessions, social media content, automation consulting
- Access to buyer group: 15-20 local animal hospitals + national network of thousands
- Domain owned: jpdigitalworks.com — no website built yet
- No pricing or packages defined
- No paying clients yet (revenue starts with buyer group clients, not HPAH)

## 90-Day Goal

**3-5 paying veterinary practice clients on monthly retainers, with a live website and a repeatable sales process through buyer group introductions.**

## Positioning

The Insider Partner + Not Software, Service: AI-powered business services for veterinary practices, built inside a real animal hospital. Not another software subscription — a hands-on partner who delivers results and trains your team.

## Services

1. **SEO/AEO Monthly Reports** — Done-for-you, monthly recurring
2. **Social Media Content Creation** — Done-for-you content, they post it
3. **AI Training & Automation Consulting** — Done-with-you, session + project-based
4. **AI Technology Updates** — Ongoing advisory (relationship builder)

## Target Audience

Independent veterinary practices and animal hospitals (2-20 employees). Practice owners and office managers who know they need better marketing and AI adoption but don't have the time or knowledge to do it themselves. Accessible through buyer group networks where peer recommendations drive purchasing decisions.

# Phase 1: Build the Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

**Focus:** Keep delivering for HPAH, set pricing for new clients, build your case study, launch your website.

**Theme:** You can't sell what you haven't packaged. Right now you're doing great work for HPAH but you have no website, no pricing, and no way for a referred practice to say "yes." Phase 1 fixes that. HPAH stays free — they're your founding partner, not your first invoice.

---

### Week 1: Package Your Services

| Task | Deliverable |
|------|-------------|
| Define 2-3 service packages with pricing tiers | Pricing sheet with clear packages (e.g., SEO/AEO only, SEO + Content, Full AI Partner) |
| Research what vet marketing agencies and AI consultants charge | Competitive pricing doc — know where you sit vs GrowDVM ($1,500-3K/mo), generic AI agencies ($2K-8K/mo) |
| Draft a simple one-page service overview (not a full website — just a PDF or doc you can send) | One-pager you can email or text to a buyer group contact tomorrow if needed |

**Week 1 Milestone:** You have packages with prices ready for new clients. HPAH continues as your free founding partner — their value is the testing ground, case study, and buyer group access, not a monthly payment.

**Pricing guidance:** Based on competitive research, consider:
- **Starter:** SEO/AEO Monthly Reports only — $500-800/month
- **Growth:** SEO/AEO + Social Media Content — $1,000-1,500/month
- **Partner:** Full suite (SEO/AEO + Content + AI Training + Automation Consulting) — $2,000-3,000/month
- **AI Training:** One-time or quarterly sessions — $500-1,000 per session

These are below the vet marketing agency range ($1,500-5,000/mo) but above what a freelancer would charge. Your AI-powered workflow lets you deliver agency-level results at these prices and still make strong margins because your time per client is low.

**Theme:** You have the proof, you have the packaging, you have the distribution channel. Now work the channel. Every conversation is a learning opportunity — what resonates, what questions they ask, what objections come up.

**How to approach the buyer group:** This is not a cold pitch. HPAH is vouching for you. The framing is: "We helped HPAH with [specific results]. They thought other practices in the group might benefit from the same thing. Here's what we do." Let HPAH do most of the talking if possible — peer recommendation is 10x more powerful than your own pitch.

Don't give them the full action plan — just enough to prove you know what you're talking about and that there's real work to do. "Here's what I found in 30 minutes. The full monthly service goes deeper and includes the action plan + content creation."

**Phase 2 Gate:** Before moving to Phase 3, you should have at least 2 paying clients beyond HPAH. If you have 0, something is off — revisit pricing, messaging, or the buyer group approach. See Early Warning Signs below.

**Focus:** Standardize your delivery, build recurring revenue habits, plant seeds for the national buyer group, and decide if this is going full-time.

**Theme:** You've proven the model works. Now make it repeatable so it doesn't burn you out as a side hustle. Every client should get the same quality without you reinventing the process each time.

---

| Task | Deliverable |
|------|-------------|

**Weeks 9-10 Milestone:** You can onboard a new client and deliver the first month of service without scrambling. The process is documented and repeatable.

### Weeks 11-12: Plant Seeds for Growth

| Task | Deliverable |
|------|-------------|

**The full-time math:** If you're charging $1,000-2,000/month per client and each client takes 5-8 hours/month of your time, then:
- 5 clients = $5,000-10,000/month (part-time)
- 10 clients = $10,000-20,000/month (full-time viable)
- 20 clients = $20,000-40,000/month (growing business)

The buyer group gives you a path to 20+ without cold outreach. The national network gives you a path to 100+.

## Early Warning Signs

### Week 2: HPAH Results Aren't Tangible Enough for a Case Study
- **Red flag:** You've delivered work but can't point to specific before/after improvements
- **Likely problem:** The SEO/AEO changes haven't had time to show impact, or you didn't capture clear baseline metrics
- **Fix:** Run a baseline report NOW if you haven't already. Even early wins count — "we identified 14 SEO issues and fixed 8 in the first month" is a valid proof point. You don't need dramatic revenue numbers yet, just evidence of action and progress.

### Week 4: No Website Live
- **Red flag:** You're overthinking the design, the name, the copy
- **Likely problem:** Perfectionism. The website doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to exist
- **Fix:** Give yourself 4 hours total. One page. Use a template. Ship it. You can improve it later — you can't improve what doesn't exist

### Week 6: Buyer Group Isn't Responsive
- **Red flag:** HPAH made the intro but nobody's biting
- **Likely problem:** The group might not meet regularly, or the intro was too passive (email forward vs in-person vouching)
- **Fix:** Ask HPAH to bring you to the next in-person meeting or call. Offer to present for 10 minutes. If there's no regular meeting, ask HPAH to make 3-5 individual warm intros to specific practices they know well

### Week 8: Discovery Calls But No Closes
- **Red flag:** Practices are interested but nobody signs up
- **Likely problem:** Either pricing is off, the proposal is too vague, or they don't see enough proof
- **Fix:** Offer the free SEO snapshot if you haven't. Drop the price for the first 2 clients to get case studies. Ask directly: "What would need to be true for this to be a yes?" — their answer tells you exactly what's missing

### Week 12: Clients Are Paying But It's Eating All Your Time
- **Red flag:** You're spending 15+ hours per client per month
- **Likely problem:** You haven't systemized your delivery — every client feels like starting from scratch
- **Fix:** Template everything. Your Claude prompts, your report format, your content creation workflow. The AI should be doing the heavy lifting — you should be reviewing and delivering, not creating from scratch each time

## Competitive Advantages to Protect

1. **Insider access** — Your wife works at HPAH. You understand how a vet practice actually runs from the inside. No marketing agency or SaaS company has this. Don't lose this advantage by getting too "agency-like" in your communication.

2. **Buyer group distribution** — This is a moat. Getting introduced through the buyer group means zero cold outreach and instant credibility. Protect this by making HPAH wildly successful — their results are your sales pitch.

3. **AI-native delivery** — You use Claude as a force multiplier, which means you can deliver agency-quality work at lower prices with higher margins. Don't compete on price alone — compete on results + price. But know that your cost structure is a structural advantage.

4. **Vet-specific positioning** — Nobody else is doing hands-on AI business automation specifically for vet practices. The generic AI agencies serve everyone. The vet marketing agencies do traditional marketing. You're in the white space. Stay there until you dominate it.

5. **Personal relationships** — This is a referral business. Every client becomes a potential referrer. Prioritize relationships over scale in the first 90 days. One delighted practice owner who tells 5 friends is worth more than any marketing campaign.

6. **Staying current** — You genuinely love AI and stay on the bleeding edge. This is rare and valuable. Your clients will never have to worry about using outdated tools because you're always testing what's new.

## Naming Notes

The name decision should happen in Week 3 at the latest — you need it for the website. Options to consider:

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Keep JP DigitalWorks** | Domain owned, no cost, easy | Sounds dated, doesn't signal vet expertise |
| **Rebrand vet-specific** | Immediately signals who you serve, memorable in buyer group context | Limits you if you expand beyond vet later |
| **Rebrand modern/broad** | Fresh name, room to grow into other verticals | Doesn't signal vet niche |
| **Use your personal name** | Trust-based selling (referral networks buy from people), simple | Harder to scale/sell the business someday |

**Recommendation:** If you're going vet-niche-first (which the buyer group strategy demands), lean toward something that signals you understand their world — even subtly. You can always create a parent brand later when you expand to other verticals. The name your buyer group contact hears matters.
