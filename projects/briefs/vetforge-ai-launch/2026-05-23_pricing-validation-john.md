# Pricing Validation — Conversation Guide for John (HPAH Practice Manager)

**Created:** 2026-05-23
**Purpose:** Get honest, insider willingness-to-pay data and an offer critique from John — a real practice manager who's helping on the business side. Validate the tier prices *before* taking them to paying buyer-group clients.
**Status:** Working doc — fill in answers during/after the conversation.

---

## How to use this (read first)

- **This is a conversation, not a survey.** Walk through it with John. Let him talk; capture what he says verbatim where it's useful.
- **Be straight with him about the goal.** "HPAH isn't paying — you're my beta. But you're exactly the person I'd be selling this to, so I want your honest read on what a practice like yours would actually pay, and where the offer is weak."
- **Don't lead the price.** For the Van Westendorp section especially, let *him* say the numbers before you show yours. If you anchor him with your prices first, his answers just echo you.
- **One person is directional, not statistical.** John gives you a strong first read. Run the same four price questions past 3–5 more buyer-group contacts later to get a real range.

## Section 2 — Price sensitivity (Van Westendorp)

Describe the **core service** plainly, then ask the four questions. Let him answer with numbers before showing him anything.

> *"A done-for-you service: I run your website, handle your SEO so you show up on Google, keep you showing up when people ask AI tools like ChatGPT for a vet, and produce your social media content — all in your practice's voice. Your involvement is about 15 minutes a month."*

1. At what **monthly price** would this be so cheap you'd question whether it's any good? _(too cheap)_
   - _Answer: $____/mo_
2. At what monthly price would you consider it a **bargain** — great value for the money? _(bargain)_
   - _Answer: $____/mo_
3. At what monthly price would it start to feel **expensive** — you'd have to think about it? _(getting expensive)_
   - _Answer: $____/mo_
4. At what monthly price would it be **too expensive** to even consider? _(too expensive)_
   - _Answer: $____/mo_

**Reading it:** the acceptable range sits roughly between his "too cheap" and "too expensive" answers; the sweet spot is near where "bargain" and "getting expensive" meet. Compare that range to your $600 / $1,200 / $2,200 tiers.

## Section 3 — Reaction to the actual tiers

*Now* show him the real packaging. For each tier, get a gut reaction and the "why."

| Tier | Price | Reaction (no-brainer / fair / a stretch / too much) | Why? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spark** (your dev implements, we do strategy + content) | $600/mo | | |
| **Forge** (we run your whole website + AI search + content) ★ | $1,200/mo | | |
| **Foundry** (everything + automation + strategy) | $2,200/mo | | |

Then:
- **Which tier would HPAH pick if they were paying?** Why that one?
- **At Forge's $1,200 — too high, too low, or right?** (This is your anchor tier.)
- **What would you add to make it an obvious yes? What would you cut?**
- **Is anything missing that you'd expect for the price?**

## Section 6 — The offer & the buyer group (John's insider read)

This is where John is worth the most. Let him be blunt.

1. As a practice manager, what would make you say **yes** to this? What would make you hesitate?
2. What would make a peer in your buyer group **recommend it** to another practice? What kills a recommendation?
3. How does buying actually work in your group — who decides, who has budget, what's the process?
4. Is "I built this inside a real animal hospital" actually persuasive, or does it need more?
5. What's confusing, missing, or over-complicated about the way this is packaged?
6. If you were pitching this for me at a buyer-group meeting, what would you say?
